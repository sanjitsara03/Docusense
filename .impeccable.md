## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters and hiring managers viewing a portfolio project linked from a resume or LinkedIn. They're not technical — they need to be impressed in ~10 seconds. The interface must communicate sophistication and intentionality at a glance. Secondary audience: senior engineers who will evaluate the ML architecture through the quality of the demo.

### Brand Personality
Refined, precise, understated. Three words: **calibrated, architectural, composed**.

Not loud. Not trying too hard. The kind of interface that signals the engineer cares about craft at every layer — not just the model.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Dark mode, warm-neutral palette.** Not the typical AI product aesthetic (no cyan, no purple-to-blue gradients, no glowing cards). Instead: deep charcoal backgrounds with warm undertones, cream/parchment accents that echo the physicality of documents, a single sharp accent color (warm amber or antique gold) used sparingly for active states.

**Typography-led layout.** Documents are about text — lean into that. Use a distinguished serif display font (Cormorant Garamond or Playfair Display) for classification results and headings, paired with a refined geometric sans (DM Sans or Outfit) for body copy and metadata. Type should feel editorial, not developer-toolish.

**Architecture over decoration.** No glass cards, no rounded-everything, no icon-above-heading rows. Layouts should feel like they were designed with intention — asymmetric where asymmetry adds meaning, tight where tightness creates focus.

**The heatmap moment is the hero.** When results appear, the Grad-CAM overlay should feel like a reveal — a document laid out on a table with light falling on it, the hot regions glowing naturally from the image. This is the thing someone will remember.

Anti-references: corporate SaaS blue-button-white-card layouts. Generic AI product purple/cyan gradients.

### Design Principles
1. **Impress at 10 seconds** — the above-the-fold state (upload zone + branding) must communicate the product's intelligence before any interaction.
2. **The document is the protagonist** — the uploaded image and heatmap overlay should dominate the results view; the extracted fields are supporting cast.
3. **Confidence deserves presence** — the confidence score is not metadata, it's a first-class result. Display it with weight and precision.
4. **Stillness over noise** — use motion only at reveal moments (classification result appearing, fields populating). Default state should feel calm and composed.
5. **Warmth from the palette, not personality** — the interface is a tool, not a character. Warmth comes from color temperature choices, not playful copy or emoji.
